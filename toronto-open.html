The story

The Toronto Open started in September 2023 with eight firms, one borrowed trophy, and a single question — what does a corporate event look like when nobody on Bay Street has ever done it before?

Three years later, the Cup has its own engraver, its own anthem, and its own rivalries. Twelve firms compete every September. A defending champion goes home with the trophy on Friday night and brings it back the following year. The losing firms write a cheque to a charity of the winner's choice.

It is, as far as we know, the only invitational of its kind in North America. And we'd like to keep it that way.

The trophy

The Bay Street Cup.

Hand-cast in a Hamilton foundry. Sterling silver and 18-karat gold leaf. Eighty-three names engraved at the base, growing each September. The defending champion keeps it for fifty-one weeks, in a glass case in their lobby. They give it back the morning the next Open begins.

It weighs nineteen pounds. Two of the three permanent rules of the Toronto Open concern the trophy.
